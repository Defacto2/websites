Wednesday 20th May (7:25pm GMT)

You thought your Diamond Monster 2 12meg rocked? Well a new range of Voodoo2 cards from Obsidian will be hitting a store near you soon. What makes these Voodoo2 Obsidians so special? Well for starters the top of the line model called to Obsidian2 x-24 supports SBSLI (Single Board Scan Line Interleaving) with dual chips on board. It has 24megs of memory and get this, it has six Voodoo2 processors running in parallel. As for benchmarks, Quake 2's timedemo 1 at 1024*768 on a P2 333 ran at an amazing 84.4fps! Oh and lets not forget about the price, one of these babies will cost around $700us.

Wednesday 13th May (5:40pm GMT)

Ionizer's The Game Review has been delayed and now has a returning date of June the 7th. The Game Review happens to be the longest surviving scene publication and at one time was voted best newletter/electronic magazine in the scene.

This Friday (Saturday morning GMT) should see the launch of a new gaming publication called the Game Over Online Magazine. This new publication is a joint venture between The Reviewers Guild and The Gamers Edge. Game Over will feature news items and features on both the warez scene and the bigger stories in the gaming scene. Another big feature of the publication will be the online gaming reviews which will be a lot shorter and more to the point compaired to professional online publications.
There is no news yet on what will happen to TRG and TGE, but if you want to be the first to visit this new project goto www.game-over.net.
